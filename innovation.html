Now that many more countries do have high-speed wireless Internet, SMS is competing with messaging apps like WhatsApp and WeChat.
remains a common form of messaging since it works between any two phones on any network; no app download needed!

Just like email, SMS was originally intended for one-to-one communication, but it's now used as a way
for governments and businesses to broadcast messages to many phones at once.

A government agency can send SMS messages to subscribed residents with alerts about missing persons, severe weather, and major road closures.

Alerts from the Berkeley Police Department and Alameda County.

Similarly (though not quite as life-saving!), businesses can send SMS messages to customers to entice them with promotions.

Mobile phone users are a captive audience; the average US smartphone user touches their phones more than 2,000 times a day and the average open rate for SMS messages is as high as 98%.
